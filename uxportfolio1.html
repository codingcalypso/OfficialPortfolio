Portfolio Project 1:

Design a Mobile App

Overview:


Project Duration: April 15th, 2023- April 24th, 2023


This was the first project in the Google UX Design Professional Certificate program. It was to feature a hypothetical business or organization and I was to design a mobile app for them. Sharpen.Design was used to generate various prompts to choose from. I selected "Design an app for an art gallery".

Challenge

The initial challenge is that there are a lack of online apps for smaller art galleries to exhibit their exhibits.

The second challenge was to ensure that those with visual or physical disabilities could view the online app easily.

Solution

My solution wasn't simply to create an app for an art gallery. I also wanted to focus on those individuals who are unable to attend art galleries due to various disabilities. I focused on image sizes, clean and clear designs, and easy to click buttons and links.

My Role

I led the overall user experience and front-end development. My responsibilities included user research, wireframing/prototyping, testing, and design.

Strategy:

Being mobile friendly was my only concern at the moment. This meant prioritizing ideas and designs that fit on mobile phones specifically, not expanding designs to other screen sizes.

The research was done through peer reviewed assignments. I would submit my Google template that featured my questions and users would review it, answer the questions, and then grade my assignment. I then input their information in my templates for research where I created two individual personas, a research study plan, and user journey map.

All of these assignments are reviewable and are located in my Google Docs Case Study.

Goals:

Since I set the goals myself, I used my created personas to determine what I thought each person would like to see. I also reviewed my User Journey Map and Research Study Plan from my Google Docs Case Study to help refine my goals. I set the goals of this project as the following:

  1. Clean, efficient design
  2. Accessible to visual and physical disabilities
  3. Being welcoming and informative
  4. Allowing limited personalization

Design:

The design grew as I went through the design course. It started as a simple, 3 slide presentation and grew into 7 slides. I wanted to have a little fun and design the app in a way that allowed users to "favorite" and "share" exhibits and have their own profile that featured the exhibits they favorited. It could help show off a little bit of the users personality but I also thought these could also be considered "conversation starters" so users could eventually connect with eachother in a few different ways within the app. Unfortunately, I didn't have the skills to figure out how that could be made into a reality, but the thought was there.

To view the prototypes, please visit my Figma page and view the designs on an Iphone 14 device.

Impact:

While I don't have any official stats, given that this is all fictional, I will say I think the impact would be good for the art gallery. Many galleries are small and don't have the technical resources to build their own app/website let alone update it when new exhibits come to their gallery. I think more of these would not only benefit viewers who are unable to visit many art galleries in person, but it would give these art galleries more of an audience and then hopefully, more support for the artists.

What I Learned:

One of the most impactful things I learned during this part of the program was how important it is to design with the user in the front of my mind. Even though I thought I was always an empathetic designer, I quickly realized how often I actually designed with my own implicit biases. This was an immediate focus for me to prioritize growth.

Some other things I learned were beginner design creation in Figma, how to communicate design and development challenges with my team (aka my peers), and how important the research process is, not only before you begin designing, but through repeated testing and refining.
