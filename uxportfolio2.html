Portfolio Project 2:

Design a Responsive Website

Overview:


Project Duration: April 25, 2023 - April 29, 2023


This was the second project in the Google UX Design Professional Certificate program. This project built on the previous one by having me create a responsive website for the fictional company I built the mobile app for. The course description was to create a website with the Figma frame "Desktop 1440x1024". As a reminder, my prompt was "Design an app for an art gallery".

Challenge

The initial challenge was deciding if what I had on the mobile app should go on the website as well. The other challenges would be deciding on the design (working on consistency across platforms), deciding to add a footer with all of the links and gallery information, and trying to design more with empathy and accessibility in mind.

Solution

While I kept the pages the same as the mobile app, I made the images and text a little larger on the website. I wanted to make sure everything was easy to see, especially if users had visual disabilities. That is also why I implemented the footer because it also had all of the information available for people to read through quickly.

My Role

My role, the same was before, was that I led the overall user experience and front-end development. My responsibilities included user research, wireframing/prototyping, testing, and design.

Strategy:

I tried to focus more on the website being responsive (although I was not as successful as I would prefer) and prioritizing a clear and concise website for users. I didn't want the website to be something people had to really think through to utilize. I wanted their thoughts to be on enjoying the exhibits.

I again leaned on my two individual personas I created along with my research to continue building this website.

All of these assignments are reviewable and are located in my Google Docs Case Study.

Goals:

Since the goals were set myself, I referred back to my created personas to assist in creating my goals.

  1. Focus on peer reviewed/persona pain points
  2. Clear and concise design
  3. Keeping design consistent with mobile app

Design:

I decided to change up the colors for this design but in hindsight, I would have preferred to re-work my mobile design colors. My thinking was that I would take the gold font from the mobile app, lighten it up and make it more pastel, and make that the background color so it would be easier on the eyes than a white background. I then chose to make the purple button colors from the mobile app the main color on my website. My thought process again, still focused on being a comfortable and concise website and not being flashy enough that it detracted from the exhibits - the real star.

Impact:

I hope the impact of this website would be to encourage more celebration and importance put on art - both past and present. From a business standpoint, I believe this website would benefit an art gallery because it would allow artists a bigger platform than simply showing their art in a physical location, but it would also provde the gallery itself a larger platform and more opportunities to interact with fans and find new ways to support their business.

What I Learned:

This project was done immediately after the mobile app. I put more focus on empathetic designs by creating pages that were requested from my peers through the individual assignments. I also put more effort into creating personas and working harder to provide appropriate research (even if it is fabricated). The users were my main focus and I hope it is seen in this design.

In hindsight, like I mentioned before, I think the colors could use a little more to better match the mobile app and perhaps some user flow tweaks. But, I am still learning Figma and I couldn't quite figure out how to do a few things I wanted to do (like a "messages" page that features new exhibits or messages from another user. Is that even possible in Figma?).
