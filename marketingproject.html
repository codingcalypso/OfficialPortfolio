Marketing Campaign

Overview:


Project Duration: November 25th, 2024- December 5th, 2024


This was a creative project for a marketing campaign with the goal of launching a new Decision Intelligence Platform, Data on Demand. This initiative involved creating the assets for promoting and executing the campaign and ensuring consistent designs across multiple platforms. The actionable items I created included: a Press Release, a Company Slide Deck, a Linkedin Carousel, a Featured Article, and three Email Newsletter templates.

The company name was changed to a generic name and the information is purposefully without specific detail about the company or competitors.

Challenge

The initial challenge was being given information about a topic I was unfamiliar with (AI, the restaurant industry) so I was required to do immediate research into these topics to learn more and ensure I had a solid foundation before beginning this project.

The second challenge was to ensure that my communication was clear and my designs were professional, visually compelling, informative, and value-driven.

Solution

My solution was to emphasize on the impact that data has on the restaurant industry. I chose to focus on three main aspects:

1) What the product can do

2) Who the product can help

3) The solution this product provides

By focusing on answering these questions with my creations, I would ensure that the documents presented the appropriate information.

My Role

I self-managed this entire project from start to finish. I performed the research, designed the material, and typed up the content both from external sources and leveraging the usage of AI to assist with the flow of the material and formatting.

The project was created in a way that allowed for full creative control and decision-making. We were given the opportunity to showcase our confidence and creativity in our content and designs.

Goals:

  • Is it clear that research was done on the topics of data intelligence, data management, analytics, restaurants, audience pricing?
  • Does the work show excitability from the creator about their product?
  • Is the message clear?
  • What assets did you choose to create and are they realistic for a promotion effort?
  • Did you design something unique?
  • Are the assets clean and easy to follow?
  • What is the overall grasp of what this product offers?
  • How well were the tasks executed?

Strategy:

The research began with researching competitors to the product. I focused on what they were offering but also how they were advertising their product.

From there, I began choosing my actionable items to create and begin prep work for each. I leveraged ChatGPT to discuss what my ideas were and if they fell in line with marketing proposals.

Finally, I organized each asset by picking one topic to touch on for each document, paying attention to not replicate the content on multiple creations.

Design:

The designs were created utilizing Canva for ease of use but also the convenience of quick adjustments that would be needed. The company would need creations quickly but also needed to ensure that edits could be done at a moments notice and editable by multiple people.

I chose included a main color palette of red, gold, and tan. I felt the colors were bold enough to stand out but not too bold that they took away from the professionalism of the creations. I was also able to add in shades of these main colors for emphasis in the designs.

The design of the assets themselves were based on how quickly and efficiently I could showcase my topics. I had specific bullet points for each document and I wanted to make sure the content showcased the value of the product, was visually enjoyable, but also impactful with what the product could do for the consumers .

Impact:

Overall, the material was well-received by the hiring team with emphasis placed on the professionally designed creative pieces and properly showcasing the importance of data that this product offered.

The choice of assets were also praised as they hit the preferred advertising platforms and were convenient for the sales team to advertise as they were given multiple choices to present the information to clients.

What I Learned:

One of the most impactful things I learned during this part of the program was how important it is to ensure proper documentation of impactful areas of the product.

I wasn't simply creating a document to "sell" an item. I was creating a document that placed hard-hitting, value driven information to an audience that needed to quickly understand the impact it would have on their business.

I knew that if I didn't hit the target, then it cause a negative ripple effect for the sales team, the customer service team, and the product team.

Another thing I learned was how to utilize ChatGPT in a way that didn't just "give me templates" to copy but to use it as a foundation for content creation then edit the verbiage to include the details about the company and documentation that I needed. I was able to ask multiple questions and parse down their assistance to eventually end up with content that was focused, clear, and impactful.
